Fettucine Alfredo

recipe and instructions

servings: 4 | ingredients: 7ish | prep time: 30 min

This is a super basic recipe for making alfredo sauce while cooking fettucine for it to go on. (It does assume you know how to follow instructions for cooking pasta from a box.)

If you want to attempt doing three things at once, saute some vegetables while you're making the sauce and waiting for the noodles to cook. Fettucine alfredo is good topped with mushrooms, asparagus, broccoli, bell peppers, eggplant, or probably whatever sounds like a good idea to you.

  • 2 pounds fettucine noodles
  • 2 cups grated parmesan cheese
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 1/2 cups half & half
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • salt & pepper to taste
  • chopped parsley or basil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a pot large enough to cook 2 pounds of pasta. Start making the sauce once you add the pasta to the water.
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat and saute the garlic until lightly browned.
  3. Slowly add the half & half while stirring.
  4. When the liquid is simmering, slowly whisk in Parmesan cheese and stir until it dissolves.
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Continue stirring until the sauce thickens to desired consistency, while checking to make sure you haven't overcooked the fettucine.
  7. Serve immediately. Add basil or parsley to the top for added flair and taste.
